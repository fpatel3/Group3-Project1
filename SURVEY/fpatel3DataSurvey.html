BIG DATA SURVEY

Big Data is a term used to describe data sets so large, so
complex or that require such rapid processing (sometimes
called the Volume/Variety/Velocity problem), that they
become difficult or impossible to work with using standard
database management or analytical tools. Manipulating data
sets like these often require massively parallel software 
running on tens, hundreds, or even thousands of servers.    
        
Big Data growth includes the explosion of social media,
video, photos, unstructured text in addition to the data
gathered by ubiquitous sensing devices including smart
phones. Among the many difficulties associated with Big
Data are capture, storage, search, sharing, analysis, 
and data visualization.
